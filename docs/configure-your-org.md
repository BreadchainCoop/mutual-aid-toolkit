# Configure your org

Everything about how your org looks and which tools it uses lives in **Settings**
— open it from the nav (⚙️). Because your community's identity lives inside your
own copy of the data, a change you save here **syncs to every device on your
team**. No config file, nothing to rebuild.

> Only **admins** can change settings. Volunteers see the same screen read-only.

![Configuring your org](gifs/create-org.gif)

## Identity

| Field | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Community name** | Shows in the top bar and the browser tab, and on your team's roster. |
| **Short name / initials** | Used for the little logo chip if you pick the *Initials* logo, e.g. *AMA*. |

Renaming your org updates it everywhere — the top bar, the tab title, and the
roster heading — for everyone, the next time their app syncs.

## Look

| Field | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Brand color** | Recolors the top bar, buttons and highlights. |
| **Accent color** | The secondary color used for accents. |
| **Logo** | *Bread mark* (the default), *Initials chip* (your short name), or *No logo*. |

## Tools your org uses

Every community gives out different things and works differently. Turn off the
tools you don't need and they disappear from the menu — for everyone.

You can toggle: **Appointments**, **Outreach**, **Furniture**, **Social
services**, **Distros & no-shows**, and the **Dashboard**. The essentials —
**Check-in**, **Intake**, **Look up**, and **your team** (Roster) plus Settings
— always stay on.

Turning a tool back on brings it back exactly as it was; nothing is deleted.

## What's still set at build time

A few things are baked into your copy of the toolkit when it's built, not edited
live:

- **The catalog** — the exact list of goods, social services and languages. See
  [Make it your own](make-it-your-own.md) for how to ship your own catalog.
- **The default theme** — the colors a brand-new org starts with, before anyone
  opens Settings.

## Syncing & relays

New orgs sync through the **maintainers' community relay** by default, so you can
invite your team right away — there's nothing to set up. A relay only shuttles
your (already access-controlled) updates between devices you've put on the
roster; it can't read or change your data on its own.

For anything sensitive, run your own relay instead of the shared one — see
[Run your own relay](self-host-relay.md). You set a custom relay under
**Advanced** on the create/join screen, or leave the relay blank to keep an org
on a single device with no sync at all.
