# Quickstart

From opening the app to your first distribution, in about ten minutes. Nothing
you do here leaves your device until you invite someone.

## 1. Open it and start your org

Open the app. Choose **Create a new org on this device** and set:

- **Community name** (e.g. *Anytown Mutual Aid*)
- **Short name / initials**, **brand color**, and a **logo** — or take the
  defaults; you can change all of this later in **Settings**.
- **Your device name** (e.g. *Rosa — laptop*). You become the first admin.

New orgs sync through the community relay automatically, so you can invite your
team right away. (Prefer your own relay, or offline-only? Open **Advanced: sync
relay** — see [Run your own relay](self-host-relay.md).)

Hit **Create**. The app immediately wears your name and colors.

## 2. Make it yours (optional, 1 minute)

Open **Settings** (⚙️). Rename the org, adjust the colors and logo, and turn off
any tools you won't use — they vanish from the menu for everyone. Changes sync to
your whole team. See [Configure your org](configure-your-org.md).

## 3. Invite your team

Open **your team** (Roster) → **QR invite**. Give it a name, show the code (or
copy the link). Volunteers scan it, type their name, and they're in — as
volunteers. Promote anyone to admin from the members list. Full details:
[Invite people & manage your team](invite-and-manage.md).

## 4. Take some intake

Open **Intake**. Add a household — who they are, the languages they speak, and
what they need. Each submission becomes a household with open requests.

## 5. Run the day

- **Outreach** — build a call list and text people in their language.
- **Check-in** — on distribution day, find someone by the **last 4 digits** of
  their phone, and mark what they received.
- **Distros** — schedule the event and run the no-show pass afterward.
- **Dashboard** — see what's most needed, ranked.

The whole workflow is walked through in [Run a
distribution](run-a-distribution.md).
